Core Beliefs of the Eastern Catholic Church

The Eastern Catholic Churches are ancient Christian communities that share the same Catholic faith while preserving the spiritual, theological, and liturgical traditions of the Christian East. They are in full communion with the Bishop of Rome, yet they maintain their own ancient rites, customs, and forms of worship.

The Eastern Catholic Churches are ancient Christian communities that share the same Catholic faith while preserving the spiritual, theological, and liturgical traditions of the Christian East. They are in full communion with the Bishop of Rome, yet they maintain their own ancient rites, customs, and forms of worship.

1 Belief in the Holy Trinity

Eastern Catholics believe in one God in three Persons: the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit. The Holy Trinity is the eternal mystery of divine life, perfect unity, and perfect love.

2 Faith in Jesus Christ

Jesus Christ is believed to be the eternal Son of God who became man for our salvation. He is fully God and fully man. Through His incarnation, death on the cross, and glorious resurrection, He conquered sin and death and opened the way to eternal life.

3 Sacred Scripture and Holy Tradition

Eastern Catholics receive both Sacred Scripture and Holy Tradition as the living deposit of the faith handed down from the Apostles. The Bible is read within the life of the Church, guided by the worship, teachings, and witness of the saints.

4 The Seven Holy Mysteries

The Church celebrates seven Holy Mysteries, also known in the West as the sacraments. These are sacred means by which the grace of God is given to His people:

  • Baptism
  • Chrismation
  • Holy Eucharist
  • Confession
  • Holy Matrimony
  • Holy Orders
  • Anointing of the Sick

5 The Divine Liturgy and the Eucharist

The heart of Eastern Catholic worship is the Divine Liturgy. In the Holy Eucharist, the faithful receive the true Body and Blood of Christ. The Liturgy is understood as participation in heavenly worship and as the central act of the Church’s life.

6 Salvation and Theosis

A central teaching of Eastern Christianity is theosis, meaning divinization or participation in the life of God. By grace, through Christ and the Holy Spirit, human beings are called not only to be forgiven but to be transformed and united more deeply with God.

7. The Mother of God and the Saints

Eastern Catholics deeply honor Mary as the Theotokos, meaning “God-bearer” or “Mother of God.” The saints are also greatly venerated as holy men and women who reflect the glory of Christ and pray for the Church.

8 The Church as the Body of Christ

The Church is the Body of Christ, guided by the Holy Spirit and founded upon the Apostles. Eastern Catholic Churches are led by bishops and, in some cases, patriarchs or major archbishops, while remaining in full communion with the Pope of Rome.

9 Prayer, Fasting, and Holy Living

The Eastern Catholic spiritual life emphasizes prayer, fasting, repentance, worship, mercy, and love for the poor. Believers are called to grow in holiness through daily conversion and faithful participation in the life of the Church.

10 Hope in the Resurrection

Eastern Catholics believe in the resurrection of the dead and the life of the world to come. The Christian life is lived in hope, looking toward the final victory of Christ and the fullness of communion with God in His Kingdom. >
